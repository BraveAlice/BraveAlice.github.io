Am I in an abusive relationship?

2021-11-19 3 min read

Everyone has arguments, and every supervisee disagrees with their supervisors from time to time.
If this begins to form a consistent pattern, then it is an indication of domestic violence and abuse.

The answers to the following questions may help you:

Has your supervisor tried to keep you from seeing other supervisors, researchers or students?

Has your supervisor prevented you or made it hard for you to continue or start researching, or froming going to conference, or froming teaching modules?

Does your supervisor unjustly accuse you of stupid, bad at language or your research knowledge?

Does your supervisor constantly belittle or humililate you, or regularly criticise or insult you?

Are you ever afraid of your supervisor?

Have you ever changed your behaviour because you are afraid of what your supervisor might do or say to you?

Has your supervisor ever change any part of your or research project deliberately?

Has your supervisor ever hurt or threatened you or your academic results (spiritual child)?

Has your supervisor ever kept you short of money so you are unable to continue research and other necessary support for yourself and your research?

Has your supervisor ever forced you to do something that you really did not want to do?

Has your supervisor ever tried to prevent you from taking necessary medication, or seeking medical help when you felt you needed it?

Has your supervisor ever tried to control you by telling you that you could be withdrawed because you need his/her approval to proceed your research?

Has your supervisor ever threatened to stop your research project, or said he would refuse to let you continue, or even to cancell them, if you dont listen to him/her?

Has your supervisor ever forced or harassed you to have sex with him/her or with other people? Has he/she made you participate in sexual activities that you were uncomfortable with?

Has your supervisor made sexual or race comments that you were uncomfortable with?

Has your supervisor ever tried to prevent your leaving the office or meeting room?

Does your supervisor blame you for his late or no feedback and guidance on your work?

These questions are inspired by
https://www.womensaid.org.uk/the-survivors-handbook/am-i-in-an-abusive-relationship/
